Biographies

Neal Parikh is a junior from Long Island, New York pursuing his Bachelor's degree in Computer Science Engineering. Prior to taking office as President, Neal served as an Undergraduate Senator for ESG, Engineering Student Represenative for Central Student Government and the Director of Leadership for the defunct University of Michigan Engineering Council. Neal is also a member of the first professional technology fraternity, Kappa Theta Pi. In his free time, Neal enjoys watching TV shows like Suits, House of Cards, or Game of Thrones, listening to music, and exploring fun and new places. Feel free to visit him during his office hours, or send him an e-mail to see how ESG can help you!

Rebekah Andrews is from Chesaning, Michigan, and is a senior in Industrial & Operations Engineering with an Environment minor. Rebekah is also involved in the aerospace community serving as Vice President for the Michigan Aeronautical Science Association, Board Administrator for Students for the Exploration and Development of Space and as a member of the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics. In her free time Rebekah enjoys being outside, eating good food, hanging out with friends and finding the best ice cream in Ann Arbor. Rebekah is passionate about improving the CoE community and would love to hear any problems or suggestions from you!

Elise Machiele, an Ann Arbor native, is a senior in Mechanical Engineering minoring in Art and Design with an Engineering Honors Concentration in Design. On campus, she is a leader in the 1931E Society, ESG, CARE: Cost Assistance and Relief for Engineers, WERLD Design club, and is involved in the Cycling Team, Pi Tau Sigma Honor Society, Shipman Society, and SWE. Passionate about the crossover between Engineering and Art & Design, she has worked as a co-op at GE Appliances and as an intern at Exodynamics Medical and 3M Company. This summer she will intern at Ford in Product Development for Body Exterior.

Basel Alghanem is a junior studying Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Physics. Basel is involved in a number of organisations on campus, including the American Society of Mechanical Engineers, the Student Space Systems Fabrication Lab, and Pi Tau Sigma. Basel also enjoys mentoring Skyline High School's FIRST Robotics team.
