Biographies

Neal Parikh is a Junior from Long Island, New York pursuing his Bachelor's degree in Computer Science Engineering. Prior to taking office as President, Parikh served as an Undergraduate Senator for ESG, Engineering Student Represenative for Central Student Government and the Director of Leadership for the defunct University of Michigan Engineering Council. Parikh is also a member of the first Professional Technology Fraternity, Kappa Theta Pi. In his free time, Neal enjoys watching TV shows like Suits, House of Cards, or Game of Thrones, listening to music, and exploring fun and new places. Feel free to visit him during his office hours, or send him an e-mail to see how ESG can help you!

David Hershey is from Bloomfield Hills, Michigan, and is currently a senior in Aerospace Engineering with a minor in Computer Science. David is active in the aerospace community, and he is currently serving at the President of Sigma Gamma Tau, the aerospace honors society. He worked for NASA Ames during the summer of 2014. David can be found on the weekends watching any Michigan sport on TV, and in the fall he has a tendency to wear a costume and sit in the front row of Michigan Football games. He also enjoys playing IM sports, exploring Ann Arbor, rock climbing, playing guitar and photography.

Elise Machiele, an Ann Arbor native, is a senior in Mechanical Engineering minoring in Art and Design with an Engineering Honors Concentration in Design. On campus, she is a leader in the 1931E Society, ESG, CARE: Cost Assistance and Relief for Engineers, WERLD Design club, and is involved in the Cycling Team, Pi Tau Sigma Honor Society, Shipman Society, and SWE. Passionate about the crossover between Engineering and Art & Design, she has worked as a co-op at GE Appliances and as an intern at Exodynamics Medical and 3M Company. This summer she will intern at Ford in Product Development for Body Exterior.

Basel Alghanem is a junior studying Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Physics. Basel is involved in a number of organisations on campus, including the American Society of Mechanical Engineers, the Student Space Systems Fabrication Lab, and Pi Tau Sigma. Basel also enjoys mentoring Skyline High School's FIRST Robotics team.
