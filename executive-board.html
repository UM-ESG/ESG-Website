Biographies

Max Olender is a senior earning his Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering and pursuing a minor in Electrical Engineering. Max is very involved in a number of engineering organizations on campus, including Pi Tau Sigma, Tau Beta Pi, the Epeians, and the Jewish Engineering Association. Max is an outdoor enthusiast who enjoys camping, hiking, biking, backpacking, climbing, and more whenever he has the opportunity.

David Hershey is from Bloomfield Hills, Michigan, and is currently a senior in Aerospace Engineering with a minor in Computer Science. David is active in the aerospace community, and he is currently serving at the academic chair of Sigma Gamma Tau, the aerospace honors society. He will be working for NASA Ames during the summer of 2014. David can be found on the weekends watching any Michigan sport on TV, and in the fall he often camped out to get front row seats for Michigan football games. He also enjoys playing IM sports, exploring Ann Arbor, rock climbing, playing guitar and photography.

Colin is a senior studying mechanical engineering with minors in multidisciplinary design and the international program in engineering. He has been involved in many student organizations including Formula Hybrid Racing, Pi Tau Sigma, Tau Beta Pi, and S3FL. In his spare time he enjoys playing soccer, volleyball, and the piano.

Self proclaimed programming aficionado, hailing from the west side of the state, Nick Babcock is here to deal with finances. With the experience of being an intern at one of the world's largest hedgefunds and also founding and running a profitable software consultancy while in high school (NBSoftSolutions), Nick knows how to handle money. Currently, he is a senior and will be graduating with a computer science engineering degree and a minor in mathematics. When not programming or studying for classes, he can found working as a senior developer for the local tech firm, Hiperlogic.
